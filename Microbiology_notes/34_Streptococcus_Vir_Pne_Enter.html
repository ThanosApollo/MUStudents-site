34. Streptococcus - Viridans streptococci, Streptococcus pneumoniae, Enterococcus

Characteristics

  • All streptococci are catalase positive

Viridans Streptococci

  • S.Mutans
  • S.Mitis
  • S.Sanguinis

Found in

  • Found in Oropharynx

Bacterial culture

  • Gram positive cocci in chains
  • Optochin resistant
  • bile insoluble

Virulence factors

  • Dextrans -> Binding on damaged heart valces
  • Biofilm formation(dental plaque) by S.Mutans and S.Mitis

Diseases

  • Dental carries -> S.mutans and S.mitis
  • Subacute endocarditis -> S.Sanguinis

Treatment

  • Penicillin or 3rd gen cephalosporins

Streptococcus Pneumonia

Found in

  • Nasopharynx

Bacterial cutlure

  • Gram positive diplococci in chains
  • Encapsulated, Lancet shaped diplococci
  • Optochin-sensitive
  • Bile soluble

Virulence factors

  • Capsule
  • IgA protease

Diseases

  • Pneumonia, with rusty colored sputum
  • Pharyngitis
  • Otitis Media
  • Meningitis

Treatment

  • Penicillin or 3rd gen cephalosporins(e.g ceftriaxone)

Enterococcus

  • E.faecium(less common, more likely to be resistant to vancomycin)
  • E.faecalis(more common, less likely to be resistant to vancomycin

Found in

  • GI tract

Bacterial culture

  • Gram positive diplococci
  • Lancfield group D
  • Alpha hemolysis and gamma hemolysis
  • Growth i 40% bile and 6.5% NaCl

Diseases

  • Usually trigerred by GI/GU procedures
  • UTIs
  • Endocarditis
  • Biliary tree infections

Treatment

  • IV Linezolid or Tigecycline
