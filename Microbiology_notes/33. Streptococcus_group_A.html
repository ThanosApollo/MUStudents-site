33. Streptococcus,Group A,B and other Beta hemolytic

Characteristics

  • All streptococci are catalase positive

Streptococcus Pyogenes-Group A

Found in

  • Oropharynx

Bacterial cutlure

  • gram positive cocci in chain arrangement
  • catalase positive
  • Bacitracin sensitive
  • Lancfield Group A

Virulence factors

  • Toxins
    • Erythrogenic exotoxin A
    • Streptolysin O(lyses leukocytes)
  • Enyzmes
    • DNase -> Destroys neutrophils
    • Streptokinase
    • Hyaluronidase
  • Proteins
    • Protein F ->mediates adherance to epithelial cells
    • M Protein -> prevents opsonization by C3b
    • Hyaluronic acid capsule -> inhibits phagocytosis
  • Diseases
    • Head and Neck
      • Pharyngitis
      • Tonsilitis
      • Otitis media
    • Skin
      • Erysipellas
      • Cellulitis
      • impetigo(honey crusted lesions)
      • Necrotizing fasciitis
    • Toxin mediated
      • Scarlet fever(Erythrogenic exotoxin A)
      • Streptococcal toxic shock-like syndrome
    • Immunologic Complications
      • Acute rheumatic fever
      • Poststreptococcal glomerulonephritis

Treatment

  • Penicillin or 2nd gen cephalosporins

Streptococcus Agalactiae - Group B

Found in

  • Primary affects babies b for babies
  • Commonly found in genital tract

Bacterial culture

  • Chain like arrangement
  • Lancfield Group B
  • Facultative anaerobe
  • CAMP factor -> enlarges hemolysis area when placed near S.Aureus
  • bacitracin resistant
  • Hippurate positive

Virulence factors

  • Capsule

Diseases

  • Neonatal meningitis & pneumonia
  • Neonatal sepsis

Treatment

  • Penicillin or 2nd generation cephalosporins

Streptococcus Gallolyticus - Other beta hemolytic

Found in

  • Found in GI tract

Bacterial cutlure

  • gram positive cocci in chain like arrangement
  • Lancfield group D
  • Beta hemolysis or gamma hemolysis

Virulence factors

  • Capsule

Diseases

  • Bacteremia
  • endocarditis
  • Colorectal Cancer

Treatment

  • Penicillin
  • 3rd generation cephalosporins
