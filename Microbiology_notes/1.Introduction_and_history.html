Introduction to medical Microbiology - History and subject

Microorganisms

  • Living, microscopic organisms
  • Require visualization through microscope
  • Clasified as:
    • Viruses(most simple)
    • Bacteria
    • Fungi
    • Parasites(most complex)

Viruses

  • Primary Classification:
    • DNA or RNA
  • Secondary Classification
    • Single or Double stranded
    • With envelope or without(naked)

Bacteria

  • Primary Classification
    • Gram positive or Gram negative(based on thickness of bacterial wall)
    • Acid fast(presence of myocolic acids) or not
  • Secondary Classification
    • Shape(e.g Rods or Cocci)
    • Aerobe or Anaerobe
    • Spore forming or not

Fungi

  • Primary Classification
    • Yeast(unicellular)
    • Mold(multicellular)

Parasites

  • Protozoa
  • Worms(helmiths)
  • Bugs(arthropods

History

  • Early life on earth was based on bacteria and archaea(prokaryotes)
  • as oxygen levels rose, eukaryotes developed, with organelles that utilized oxygen
  • Oxygen was toxic to many anaerobes, thus they developed resistance mechanisms
  • Endosymbiotic theory:
    • A prokaryotic cell capabale of englfing cells, engufled an aerobic bacterial cell
    • Host cell provided a strady environment
    • Engufled bacteria helped by generating ATP
    • This symbiosis eventually led to mitochondria
