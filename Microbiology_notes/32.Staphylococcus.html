32 Staphylococcus

Characteristics of genera Staphylococcus

  • Spherical cocci that grow in clusters
  • absence of endospores
  • nonmotile
  • Facultative anaerobic
  • gram-positive
  • catalase positive
  • present on skin and mucous membranes

S. Aureus

Found in:

  • Nostrils
  • Skin

Bacterial Culture:

  • Gram positive cocci in clusters
  • Catalase positive
  • Coagulase positive
  • Beta hemolysis
  • Mannitol fermentor
  • Yellow colonies(Aureus=Gold)

Virulence factors:

  • Enyzmes:
    • Catalase
    • Coagulase
    • Hyaluronidase
    • Lipase
    • Beta Lactamase
  • Toxins
    • Superantigens:
      • Toxic shock syndrome toxin
      • Enterotoxin B(heat stable)
    • Exfoliative Toxin
  • Proteins
    • Protein A ->Binds to Fc region of IgG, inhibiting phagocytosis
    • Modified penicillin binding proteins in MRSA(provide resistance)
  • Capsular polysaccharides

Diseases

  • Infections:
    • Skin and soft tissue infections(e.g cellulitis
    • Abscess
    • Acute bacterial endocarditis
    • Pneumonia
    • Septic Arhtritis
    • Osteomyelitis
  • Toxin mediated Diseases
    • Toxic shock syndrome
    • Food poisoning
    • Staphylococcal Skin Scalded Syndrome
  • Treatment
    • MSSA -> Oxacillin or Clindamycin
    • MRSA -> Vancomycin or Linezolid

Staphylococcus epidermidis

Found in:

  • Natural skin flora

Bacterial culture:

  • Gram positive cocci in clusters
  • Catalase positive
  • Novobiocin sensitive

Virulence factors:

  • Urease producer
  • Biofilm formation

Diseases

  • Foreign body infections(e.g Proshetic devies such as heart valves, catheters

Treatment

  • Penicillin OR Clindamycin

Staphyloccocus saprophyticus

Found in

  • Natural flora of female GI tract

Bacterial cutlure

  • Gram positive cocci in clusters
  • Novobiocin resistant

Diseases

  • Urease production

Treatment

  • 1st generation cephalosporins(e.g cephalexin)
  • Amoxicillin-clavulante
  • TMPX-SMX
